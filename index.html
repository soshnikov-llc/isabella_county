About this estimate

Why you may have seen ~11,700 before

Earlier versions used a simpler methodology — that's where ~11,710 DALYs/year came from. Three refinements changed the number. First, a longer life expectancy reference was applied, which increases the years counted as lost to premature death. Second, the mental health figures were corrected: public health surveys report how many people have ever been diagnosed with depression, not how many are actively ill today — applying a remission adjustment roughly halved that prevalence, and mental health dropped from the #1 burden to #4. Third, a statistical simulation replaced the single point estimate with a range, making the uncertainty explicit. The result is 15,430 DALYs/year — the same county, the same data sources, a more rigorous calculation. The earlier number is still available as a local planning reference in the Dashboard →
